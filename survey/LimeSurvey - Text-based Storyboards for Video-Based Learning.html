Text-based Storyboards for Video-Based Learning

This questionnaire aims to find out about your preference towards a text-based notation style in the context of creating a video-based learning module.

Audience addressed:

  • Authors of video-based learning content
  • Users of video-based learning content (teachers, educators or facilitators)
  • Researchers in the field of video-based learning / technology enhanced learning / educational technology

Please note that this survey is divided into two parts:

  1. Introduction to storyboard concept
  2. Questionnaire

Estimated time needed to complete this survey: 15 - 25 minutes.

There are 30 questions in this survey.

Efficiency of a text-based approach

After having seen the examples for text-based notations, do you agree with the opening statement that a text-based approach could be more efficient* in order to create a video-based learning module?

*Efficiency being defined as the average cost of time for creating a module (Gupta & Jalote, 2008). That is, a more time-efficient way using less time.

Examples are:

  • You would need less time than usual to create a video-based learning module.
  • The effort is perceived as lower than usual to get started and create a video-based learning module.

Effectiveness of a text-based approach

After having seen the examples for text-based notations, do you agree with the opening statement that a text-based approach could be more effective* in order to create a video-based learning module?

*Effectiveness being defined as the ability to create a module in an accurate way. That is, a module that implements the initially defined tasks correctly (Sumanth, 1994).

Examples are:

  • By formulating text you could complete the task of setting a title.
  • By formulating text you could complete the task of creating a chapter at a timestamp with a defined title.
